Read the system before prescribing the platform.

Days 0-30: Enterprise transmission map

Build a fact base across strategy, business-unit priorities, customer problems, active pilots, architecture, data, IT/OT, vendors, security, talent, adoption, and portfolio economics.

Listen

  • Chief Innovation and Growth Officer
  • Business-unit presidents and product leaders
  • Operations, IT, OT, cyber, data and finance
  • Field service, support, commercial and customers

Inspect

  • Use-case and pilot inventory
  • Cloud and edge deployment patterns
  • Data quality and access constraints
  • Vendor contracts and duplicated spend

Deliverables

Enterprise AI opportunity map; decision-rights map; current-state architecture and vendor map; adoption burden assessment; top use-case portfolio; and a concise list of where Vontier loses energy between signal and scaled outcome.

Pilot selection logic

RequiredQuestion
OutcomeIs there a specific customer or operating result worth changing?
DecisionWhat decision or action becomes better, faster, safer, or more consistent?
EvidenceWhat baseline and observable change will prove or disprove value?
AuthorityWho retains judgment, override, escalation, and risk acceptance?
OwnershipWho owns the workflow, production system, adoption, and economics?
ReuseWhat pattern, component, taxonomy, or learning can transfer?

Days 91-120

Lock the strongest proof. Compound the learning.

Production path

  • Target architecture and integration plan
  • Data, model and evaluation lifecycle
  • Cyber, compliance and validation controls
  • Monitoring, incident and rollback procedures
  • Support model and operating ownership
  • Training and adoption mechanism

Center of Excellence

  • Small core team plus virtual expert network
  • Reusable playbooks and reference architectures
  • Vendor and partner ecosystem
  • Transparent portfolio and evidence ledger
  • Community of practice tied to real work
  • Board/customer communication assets

120-day executive review

Present a portfolio with explicit scale, continue, hold, and stop decisions; one production-ready path; reusable assets; measured adoption signals; known risks; investment choices; and a one-year roadmap grounded in business-unit demand and VBS operating cadence.

Success condition

Business units experience the enterprise AI function as an accelerator that improves problem quality, reduces reinvention, makes risks visible sooner, and helps good work reach production - not as a central committee that takes ownership away.
