Re: Digital Innovation and Artificial Intelligence Leader

Dear Ms. Hasaneen,

Vontier is hiring at the point where industrial technology, connected software, AI, and enterprise operating discipline must become one system. The opportunity is not simply to generate more ideas. It is to build a repeatable mechanism that converts customer, market, machine, field, and business-unit signals into governed solutions that reach production and produce measurable value.

That is the work I have repeatedly gravitated toward. At Vape-Jet, I connect production, quality, purchasing, engineering issue flow, customer success, support, ERP/Odoo, Jira, HubSpot, Slack, telemetry, and AI-assisted workflows inside a software-enabled robotics environment. The aim is practical: cleaner handoffs, earlier risk detection, stronger standard work, and a better feedback loop from approximately 1,000 fielded machines.

My earlier work provides the industrial and operating foundation this role requires. At Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality, customer delivery, and technical programs in advanced electronics and high-reliability environments. At Amazon, I built launch and operating mechanisms under 24/7 throughput pressure. Through DudeWorth, I design AI-readiness, agentic workflow, retrieval, human-review, evaluation, and governance patterns that attach AI to decisions and work rather than novelty.

My candidate vision at https://russelldudek.github.io/Vontier/ makes the proposed approach concrete. The Innovation Torque Converter shows how Vontier could preserve business-unit speed while using a common evidence and deployment contract to move work from open discovery, through controlled coupling, into production lock-up and reusable enterprise learning. The site also includes a 120-day entry plan, role-aligned evidence map, and scenario-based operating review.

I would bring a pragmatic outside-in posture, the willingness to work directly in the problem, and respect for the operating system already inside the company. Vontier Business System should not sit beside AI innovation; it should become the mechanism that makes AI innovation repeatable, measurable, and trusted.

I welcome the opportunity to discuss where Vontier currently loses the most energy between a strong idea and a scaled outcome - and how this role can help the enterprise transmit more of it.

Sincerely,

Russell Dudek
